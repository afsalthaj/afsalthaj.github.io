The best thing I ever did in the recent years is to learn and throw myself out in the sky, by myself.

To witness something truly extraordinary, perhaps the foremost step is to feel the air by throwing yourself out, and that cannot be tandem.

An incredible soul forms the root of any complex tree of feelings. Yet we get baffled by the intricacy of what's good and what's bad, to be then amazed by the blurriness of what any of that has to do with staying true to oneself or not. That brutally gaugeable thin layer of skin always gets you to lose things — all ignited by the slight dissonance between the vibrant heart and ever pragmatic brain!

What always reverberates in my ears is that same incredible sound — "Just let it all go" before Hritik jumped onto the sky in finally a presentable Indian film made in Bollywood.

<Image src={bollywoodZnmd} alt="Zindagi Na Milegi Dobara — Just let it all go" />

To witness something truly extraordinary, perhaps the foremost step is to feel the air by throwing yourself out, and that cannot be tandem. Gazing at the distant horizon while are at the peak of sensory overload — is that point where the stress hormone halts for a significant time thereafter in life. It's a medicine.

A deliberate zoom out from anything and everything makes almost everything beautiful. A state where I don't see the details anymore, instead I see the beauty far away to be deceivingly diamonds. A state of enjoyment with zero judgements. It doesn't matter if its true or not. As Nolan says, "feel it if you don't get it"!

<Image src={skyDiveTwo} alt="Skydiving — gazing at the distant horizon" />

## Being Fearless

This weirdly beautiful world has many things boiling down to public displays and get assertion from people to finally decide who you are. We follow the weirdness while being conscious that all you get in return is the most calibrated and controlled triggers with pedantic judgements shot straight at your forehead. Being fearless is all you can do — and may be a skydive helps!
